Practice problems are for your own benefit. You won?t turn them in or have them graded, but I have the expectation that you have done these when I write my tests. You can check answers with a TA, in Math Lab, or with the professor.

\begin{enumerate}

 \item Find the general solution to $y'=y\ln(y)$, and sketch the phase line.
 
 \item Sketch a slope field for $y'=x-y$.
 
 \item Suppose $y'=-2y$ with $y(0)=50$. If you use a step size of $\Delta t$, what estimate would Euler's Method give you for $y(5)$?
 
 \item Use a computer to sketch the solutions to $y'=\frac{2x}{3y^2-1}$ for initial values $y(0)=-2,-1,0,1,2$. You can use \url{http://www.desmos.com}.
 
 \item For the solution to $y'=\frac{2x}{3y^2-1}$ with $y(0)=1$, what are the inflection points?
  
 \end{enumerate}
